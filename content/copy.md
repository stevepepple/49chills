## know.card1.title
Know before you go

## know.card1.body
There are two things we're trying to avoid: **hills and busy streets.**

Our map is focused on routes that are as comfortable, safe, and delightful as possible. That means no fast cars. As few cars as possible, actually. Your kids should feel safe on these routes. So should grandma.

The world is imperfect, so where hills and busy streets are unavoidable, we've outlined them in red. Busy streets aren't even on the map, except where you're forced to encounter them. Hills show you where to avoid — but also where to find good views.

## know.card2.title
Gear up

## know.card2.body
- In SF there's no perfect jacket, only layers. Shirt, sweatshirt, jacket, beanie, etc.
- Gloves. Trust us. We like the ones at Ornot.
- You'd look cooler in a helmet. Your hair will be fine.
- Ideal accessories: bike basket, bell, Arsicault croissant, water, this map.

## know.card3.title
On the road

## know.card3.body
- Eye contact is magic. See pedestrians and drivers, and make sure they see you. Say hi.
- Use your hands to signal turns. Good communication is a life skill and a bike skill.
- Got friends with you? Baywheels is the way. Go electric!
- Lock your bike to something metal. With a u-lock. Preferably in front of a store.
- Don't rush. Slow is forever.
